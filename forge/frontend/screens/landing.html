About the Game

What Is Forge: AI Trivia Showdown?

Forge is a free, browser-based, real-time multiplayer trivia platform powered by Google Gemini, one of the world's most capable AI language models. Unlike conventional quiz games that draw from a fixed question database, Forge synthesises completely original, never-before-seen questions on demand — every single time a game session begins.

Players connect to a shared game room using a four-character alphanumeric code. The host selects a topic and a difficulty level, and the AI generates ten calibrated questions within seconds. All participants see the same question simultaneously and have a fixed window of time to respond. Points are awarded based on accuracy and speed, creating an intense, knowledge-testing experience that rewards both recall and quick thinking.

Forge was built with a mobile-first philosophy — it runs entirely in the browser with zero installation required. The backend runs on Google Cloud Run, and real-time synchronisation uses the WebSocket protocol, giving the game sub-second response times across a global player base.

Game Flow

How a Forge Game Session Works

A complete Forge game session runs from room creation to final scoreboard in under five minutes. Here is the exact sequence of events every player experiences, from lobby to leaderboard.

1

Create or Join a Room

The host opens Forge, enters a display name, and taps Create Room. The server instantly generates a unique four-character room code. Guests enter that code and their name to join the lobby. Rooms can hold any number of concurrent players — there is no hard limit.

2

Choose a Topic and Difficulty

Only the host configures the game. They type any topic — as broad as "World History" or as niche as "1990s Bollywood Soundtracks" — and select a difficulty level (Easy, Medium, or Hard). Difficulty controls the countdown timer for each question: 30 seconds, 20 seconds, or 10 seconds respectively.

3

AI Generates Your Quiz in Real Time

When the host taps Start Game, a request is sent to the Gemini API with the chosen topic. The AI returns ten original multiple-choice questions with four answer options each, validated and shuffled before broadcast. The entire generation pipeline runs in under five seconds on average.

4

Compete Live and Track Scores

Each question is broadcast to all players simultaneously via WebSocket. A circular countdown timer ticks down in real time. Players tap one of the four colour-coded answer buttons. After every round, an answer reveal and live leaderboard flash the standings. The final game-over screen displays podium rankings, accuracy percentages, and economy rewards for signed-in accounts.

Modes

Three Game Modes for Every Occasion

Forge supports three distinct play formats, each designed around a different social context. Whether you are warming up alone before a big quiz night or coordinating a structured team battle with colleagues, there is a mode purpose-built for the situation.

🎯

SOLO MODE

SINGLE PLAYER

Solo Mode is designed for individual practice and personal improvement. There is no room code — the player creates a private session, picks a topic and difficulty, and plays through the AI-generated ten-question quiz entirely alone.

Performance is tracked locally: your accuracy percentage and final score are compared against your personal best for that difficulty tier, stored persistently in the browser. Signed-in players also earn trophy points based on how many questions they answer correctly — scoring eight or more correct earns two additional trophies per correct answer above five.

Solo Mode is the ideal warm-up before a multiplayer session, a focused study tool for a specific subject, or simply a daily brain exercise to keep general knowledge sharp.

CLASSIC MODE

MULTIPLAYER FREE-FOR-ALL

Classic Mode is the core Forge experience: every player for themselves in a synchronised, real-time quiz battle. The host creates a room, shares the code, and once all players are in the lobby, starts the game with any topic they choose.

All players receive the same questions at the same moment. Each correct answer earns points on a sliding scale from 500 to 1,000, weighted by how quickly the answer was submitted. A player who answers correctly in the first second earns the full 1,000 base points; a player who answers correctly in the final second earns 500. Incorrect answers always score zero, and unanswered questions score zero.

Signed-in players pay a small room entry fee in Forge Coins. The winner collects the pooled coins from all paid entries, creating a meaningful stakes dynamic that elevates the competitive tension in every match.

TEAM MODE

STRUCTURED TEAM BATTLE

Team Mode divides all players into two competing factions — Team A and Team B — each with a custom name. The host locks the room to freeze the roster, and players self-assign to their preferred team. Each team nominates a topic of their choice, and a randomiser wheel spins to select which topic the entire room will be quizzed on.

Individual scores still count, but a second aggregate leaderboard tracks total team points. At the end of the game, the winning team is declared alongside individual standings, making it ideal for office team-building sessions, classroom competitions, or family game nights where group collaboration and friendly rivalry need to coexist.

Scoring System

How Points and Streak Multipliers Work

Forge uses a dual-axis scoring model that rewards both accuracy and reaction speed. A correct answer always earns at least 500 base points. Answering quickly earns up to 1,000. On top of that, a consecutive-correct-answer streak system applies a multiplier to every subsequent correct answer, capping at a maximum of 3× for players on a streak of twelve or more.

This design ensures that a highly knowledgeable but slow player can still compete effectively via the streak multiplier, while a fast but inconsistent player cannot dominate purely through reflexes. The optimal strategy is accuracy first, then speed.

Difficulty

Difficulty Levels and Time Limits

Each of Forge's three difficulty settings controls one key variable: the countdown timer per question. The AI generates questions at a consistent medium-high difficulty regardless of this setting — the difficulty label refers to the time pressure applied to the player, not the complexity of the question content. This makes it trivially easy to ramp up the excitement of any session simply by lowering the timer.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Forge completely free to play?

Yes. Forge is entirely free to play for all users. There are no subscriptions, no pay-to-win mechanics, and no premium question packs. The in-game economy of Forge Coins and Trophies is earned through gameplay and does not involve real money.

Do I need to create an account to play?

Solo Mode requires no account at all — just type a name and start playing. Multiplayer Classic and Team modes require a quick Google Sign-In so that your coin balance and trophy progress can be tracked across sessions. Sign-in takes under ten seconds using any existing Google account.

How unique are the AI-generated questions?

Every game generates a completely fresh set of ten questions using Google Gemini. The AI is instructed to target medium-to-high difficulty, avoid surface-level trivia, and distribute correct answers randomly across all four answer positions. You are extremely unlikely to see the same question twice in normal gameplay.

What happens if the AI is unavailable?

Forge includes a built-in fallback question bank covering twelve topic categories. If Gemini is temporarily unavailable or takes longer than twelve seconds to respond, the server transparently uses these curated fallback questions. The host's interface shows a subtle indicator when fallback mode is active, but gameplay continues without interruption.

Does Forge work on mobile phones?

Forge was designed mobile-first. The interface is fully responsive, all tap targets meet the minimum 56-pixel touch-area standard, and the game runs smoothly on any modern Android or iOS browser. No app installation is required — open the browser, navigate to forgetrivia.online, and play instantly.

How many players can join a single room?

There is no enforced player limit per room. The server handles all connected players through asynchronous WebSocket connections, and the leaderboard displays up to eight players at a time in the in-game overlay, with full standings shown on the final results screen. Rooms with ten to twenty players are common; larger groups work fine for casual play.

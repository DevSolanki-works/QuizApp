Forge AI Trivia Topic Guide

Forge works best when the host gives the AI a clear subject, audience, and scope. This guide collects practical topic ideas, in-depth category breakdowns, and pro tips for getting the most out of AI-generated questions across every situation — from party nights to classroom sessions to solo practice.

How to Choose a Strong Topic

A good Forge topic is specific enough to produce accurate, interesting questions but broad enough to support ten rounds without running dry. Instead of asking for "movies," ask for a decade, franchise, country, award category, or theme. Instead of "science," specify the school level, branch, or concept area. The more specific your input, the more surprising and rewarding the questions will be.

Think of it this way: the AI has read everything about everything. If you give it "sports," it has to guess what angle to take. If you give it "ICC Cricket World Cup finals from 1983 to 2023," it knows exactly what pool of facts to draw from — and the questions will be richer for it.

✓ Better Topics

  • Indian cricket World Cup moments 1983–2023
  • Beginner Python programming concepts
  • Marvel Cinematic Universe phases 1 to 3
  • Class 10 biology: human body systems
  • 90s Bollywood music and composers
  • Space exploration milestones since 2000
  • Football World Cups from 1998 to 2022
  • Famous scientists and their discoveries

✗ Weak Topics

  • Sports (too broad)
  • Computer (too vague)
  • History (no scope)
  • Random hard questions
  • Movies (no era or genre)
  • Science (no level or branch)
  • India (no subject focus)
  • Music (no genre or decade)

10 Category Deep Dives

Here's a detailed breakdown of each major category — what kinds of questions to expect, which sub-topics work best, and pro tips for getting the most out of AI generation in each area.

🎬

Movies and TV Shows

Movies and television represent one of the most vibrant playgrounds for AI-generated trivia because the sheer volume of content — spanning decades, genres, and global cinema traditions — gives Gemini an immense well of interconnected details to draw from. Unlike static databases that only remember blockbuster hits, AI can pull from cult classics, foreign films, and behind-the-scenes production trivia. Expect questions about iconic quotes, directorial styles, casting trivia, release timelines, and continuity details that only true fans would know. The category rewards both casual viewers and deep cinephiles depending on the sub-topic you choose.

Marvel Cinematic Universe phases 1–3 1980s action films and directors Christopher Nolan filmography Animated Disney films 1990s
💡 PRO TIP Pair a genre with an era — e.g., "sci-fi movies from the 1990s" — which forces the AI beyond obvious choices and delivers genuinely niche questions.
🏏

Sports and Cricket

Sports trivia is ideally suited for AI because the domain is built on concrete statistics, historical records, tournament brackets, and player career arcs that are meticulously documented. Cricket in particular offers a goldmine of data — from World Cup final heroics to test match centuries and T20 league dynamics — alongside global sports like football, tennis, and basketball. Expect questions about record-breaking performances, rule nuances, underdog victories, and host nation histories. The AI excels here because sports facts are precise and verifiable, reducing the chance of hallucination.

IPL playoffs history FIFA World Cup penalty shootouts Tennis Grand Slam rivalries Olympics athletics world records
💡 PRO TIP Include "statistical or record-based" in your topic description to encourage questions about specific achievements rather than generic "who won" queries.
🔬

Science and Technology

Science and technology are natural allies of AI trivia because the field is built on universal laws, precise terminology, and a timeline of paradigm-shifting discoveries. The AI excels here because it can weave together fundamental principles, modern innovations, and the human stories behind the inventions. Expect questions about periodic table quirks, space mission milestones, biological processes, computing history, and the evolution of consumer gadgets. The category works for casual players and technical experts alike depending on the sub-topic — choose accordingly for your audience.

Space exploration 21st century Vaccine development breakthroughs History of computing hardware Periodic table elements and properties
💡 PRO TIP Request questions that ask "why" or "how" rather than just "what" — this transforms memorization into genuine conceptual understanding and makes for a richer quiz.
🌍

History and Geography

Few categories offer the narrative richness of history and geography. The AI can access timelines of empires, biographies of rulers, cultural exchanges, trade route histories, and the physical geography that shaped civilizations. Questions range from identifying ancient landmarks and treaty details to naming capital cities, geological phenomena, and key turning points in world history. To avoid overly broad or stale questions, always anchor your topic to a specific civilization, era, or geographic region — the more specific you are, the more surprising and educational the questions become.

Roman Republic to Empire transition Age of exploration Southeast Asia European river systems and trade routes World War II key battles and leaders
💡 PRO TIP Request "cause-and-effect" questions — e.g., "What was the primary economic impact of the Silk Road?" — which yields more thoughtful questions than simple date memorization.
🎵

Music and Pop Culture

Music and pop culture are dynamic, trend-driven domains where AI shines because it can access both chart-topping mainstream hits and underground niche movements. The genre spans artist biographies, album release trivia, lyric details, fashion moments, and iconic award-show incidents. Expect questions about Grammy records, one-hit wonders, musical crossovers, and the cultural impact of specific songs or albums. This category is particularly fun for mixed-age groups because every generation has a distinct musical era they know deeply — letting everyone shine at different moments in the same quiz.

90s alternative rock bands The British Invasion 1960s K-pop global rise and milestones Bollywood music composers 1970s–90s
💡 PRO TIP Ask for "artist collaboration" or "album history" angles — this forces the AI beyond surface facts and generates questions that test genuine depth of musical knowledge.
📚

Books and Literature

Literature is a treasure trove for AI trivia because it encompasses not just plot points and character names, but also literary devices, authorial intent, historical context, and intertextual references. The AI can draw from classics, modern bestsellers, poetry, and translated works from around the world. Questions you can expect include identifying a novel from its opening line, matching authors to their pseudonyms, understanding themes, and placing works in their correct literary movements. The category rewards deep readers and rewards those who go beyond the surface reading.

Victorian novels and their themes Science fiction dystopias Gabriel García Márquez works Harry Potter books 1 to 4 details
💡 PRO TIP Ask for "thematic or symbolic" questions — such as "What does the green light symbolize in The Great Gatsby?" — which challenges players to think interpretatively rather than just recall facts.
🎮

Video Games

Video games represent a modern trivia frontier that AI handles with surprising fluency, thanks to vast, well-documented wikis, lore databases, and speedrunning records available online. This category covers everything from classic arcade titles to modern open-world epics, including developer trivia, character backstories, console histories, and gameplay mechanics. You can expect questions about release dates, protagonist names, critical reception, iconic level designs, and franchise histories. This category is perfect for younger players and tech-savvy groups who grew up gaming — they'll often know details that older players won't, balancing out mixed-skill rooms.

JRPGs from the PlayStation 1 era Competitive fighting game history Indie game breakthroughs last decade Nintendo franchise history and milestones
💡 PRO TIP Specify "lore-based" or "developer-history" in your prompt — this encourages the AI to generate questions about narrative arcs and studio legacies rather than easily searchable release-year trivia.
🍕

Food and Cuisine

Food and cuisine make for a wonderfully sensory and culturally rich trivia category, blending history, chemistry, geography, and anthropology in every question. The AI can generate questions about the origins of iconic dishes, the science of cooking techniques, regional spice blends, fermentation processes, and the biographies of famous chefs. You can look forward to questions about pasta shapes, wine regions, street food origins, and bizarre food laws from around the world. This category is universally accessible — everyone eats, and everyone has opinions about food — making it an ideal warm-up category for mixed groups.

Italian regional cooking traditions Southeast Asian street food origins History of chocolate and cocoa Indian spices and their culinary uses
💡 PRO TIP Request "historical or cultural context" rather than just ingredient lists — "How did the Columbian Exchange affect European cuisine?" produces far richer questions than "What is in guacamole?"
🤖

AI, Coding and Computers

This category is delightfully meta and tailor-made for a platform like Forge, tapping into the very technology powering the game itself. AI, coding, and computers form a domain of precise terminology, algorithmic logic, hardware evolution, and the philosophy of intelligence. Expect questions about programming language syntax, famous software bugs, historical figures like Alan Turing and Ada Lovelace, neural network architectures, cybersecurity principles, and the history of the internet. This category is ideal for tech-savvy groups, CS students, and anyone curious about how the digital world actually works under the hood.

History of artificial intelligence milestones Common Python programming concepts Cryptographic history and codebreaking Famous programmers and their inventions
💡 PRO TIP Ask for "applied scenario" questions — e.g., "Which sorting algorithm is most efficient for nearly sorted data?" — which forces players to apply knowledge rather than just recite definitions.
🌐

General Knowledge

General knowledge is the classic trivia category that tests a player's overall awareness across all domains, and it works brilliantly with AI because the model can pull from any corner of its training data. The beauty of this category is its unpredictability — you might get a question about a Nobel Prize winner, followed by one about a volcanic eruption, then one about the highest-grossing animated film. While it lacks thematic focus, it is perfect for mixed-ability groups, warm-up rounds, or situations where you don't want to commit to a specific subject. The variety keeps everyone engaged and gives every player at least a few moments to shine.

World records and notable firsts Current events of the past five years Famous inventors and inventions Countries, capitals, and currencies
💡 PRO TIP Ask for "variety across at least five distinct domains" in your topic description — this ensures the AI doesn't over-index on one subject and keeps the round genuinely diverse for all players.

Topics to Avoid

Forge is built for entertainment and learning. The following topic types should not be used — not only because they violate our terms of service, but because they produce poor quiz experiences:

  • Personal information: Never enter real names, phone numbers, addresses, or private details as quiz topics. This data gets sent to a third-party AI service.
  • Harmful or hateful content: Topics designed to generate discriminatory, offensive, or dangerous content will be blocked by both our sanitization layer and Gemini's own safety filters.
  • Prompt injection attempts: Trying to trick the AI into ignoring its instructions (e.g., "ignore all previous instructions") is detected and blocked server-side.
  • Medical diagnosis or legal advice: The AI is not a licensed professional. Trivia about medical topics is fine; using it as a diagnostic tool is not appropriate.
  • Extremely obscure proper nouns: Topics like "my uncle's favourite cricket player from 1987" won't produce meaningful questions — the AI needs publicly documented subject matter to work with.
